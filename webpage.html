MOIRAI~The three goddesses of fate

Clotho

She spun the thread of life from her distaff onto her spindle. Her Roman equivalent was Nona ("the ninth"), who was originally a goddess called upon in the ninth month of pregnancy.

Lachesis

She measured the thread of life allotted to each person with her measuring rod. Her Roman equivalent was Decima ("the Tenth").

Atropos

She was the cutter of the thread of life. She chose the manner of each person's death; and when their time was come, she cut their life-thread with her abhorred shears.
